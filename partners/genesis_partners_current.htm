Research at HUMIGEN is directed towards understanding these
phenomena and initially is following four innovative lines of research:

1)  How do pathogens avoid the human immune system?

Researchers at HUMIGEN have discovered new molecules that may allow bacteria to “mop up” the messages used to activate human immunity; in so doing, these bacteria are able to grow unhindered. We are working to define the action of these new molecules and design agents that will neutralize this threat.

2)  How are immune system messengers controlled?

Here at HUMIGEN, we understand that some people make immune messengers faster than others. This allows pathogens to grow faster in some people, causing more severe diseases. Understanding the human genetic markers that predict this behavior may allow us to predict which individuals will be at greater risk in, for example, an influenza pandemic.

3)  How is immune system control disrupted in Crohn’s Disease?

Crohn’s disease, and its cousin Ulcerative Colitis, are crippling diseases of the digestive system. There are strong genetic links to these diseases, but the actual genetic variants which cause disease are not defined. At HUMIGEN, we are exploring the relationship between infections in the digestive system and genetic variants that allow the response against these infections to be turned against the bowel itself, triggering disease.

4)  How can new immune messengers modulate disease?

Recently, scientists at HUMIGEN discovered a novel control mechanism in the human immune system which, when disrupted is important cause of asthma. We are currently defining the key genetic markers in this control mechanism which make people susceptible to asthma and learning how to re-exert control in asthma patients.
HUMIGEN L.L.C., the Institute for Genetic Immunology, is a basic research company whose mission is to understand the relationship between human health, the genetics of the immune system and the development and severity of disease. Just as genetics determine common traits such as hair and eye color, they control how the human body responds to pathogens. In infection, this means that diseases may be fatal or not, or last longer or shorter. Understanding these processes will someday make a difference to treatments offered. In addition to infection, so-called “complex” or “autoimmune” diseases often occur when the immune system turns against the body. Diseases such as Lupus, Arthritis, Asthma, Psoriasis and Crohn’s disease are examples of autoimmune diseases. Understanding how immune system genetics function will bring new diagnostic and treatment options to such patients and their families. HUMIGEN is not going to solve all of these problems, but we are going to contribute to their solutions.
